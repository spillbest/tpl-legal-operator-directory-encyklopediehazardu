Lottery games (Sportka, Eurojackpot, scratch cards)

Number lotteries and scratch cards are the most widely played regulated games in the country. Draws are operated under a separate lottery permission; published odds and prize tiers are fixed by the game plan filed with the supervisory authority.

  • Draw lotteries: fixed prize tiers, published odds per tier, draws several times a week
  • Pan-European jackpot draws pooled across multiple jurisdictions
  • Scratch cards: printed and digital, with the overall payout ratio stated in the game plan

Slot machines and RNG mechanics

Every slot at a licensed casino runs on a certified random number generator; the outcome of a spin is independent of previous results. Operators must publish the return-to-player (RTP) figure for each title.

  • RTP: the long-run share of stakes returned as winnings, typically 90–97 %
  • Volatility: how unevenly that return is distributed across spins
  • Certification: testing-lab verification filed with the regulator before release

Blackjack and live tables

Blackjack offers one of the lowest house edges of any casino game when played by basic strategy. Licensed live tables display rules, limits and dealer-standing rules before you sit down.

  • Table rules that matter: dealer stands or hits on soft 17, doubling and splitting limits
  • Side bets carry a substantially higher house edge than the main hand
  • Live tables show minimum and maximum stakes per seat in the lobby

Unique casino games (Plinko, Crash Games)

Certified versions only

Drop-and-multiplier and crash-style games exist in certified versions at licensed lobbies, with published RTP and a provably random round outcome. Versions on unlicensed sites carry no such guarantees — the mechanics may look identical while the mathematics are not disclosed.
